    <h2>A system that ages with the bag.</h2>
    <p>We designed three packaging variants — house, single-origin, and reserve — that share a single grid and a single type system. The label is printed in one Pantone on uncoated kraft; the specs are stamped on at the roastery the morning the beans are bagged.</p>
    <p>The marque itself is a custom drawing built on Fraunces, with the ampersand redrawn from a 1962 ledger we found at the roastery. We held the wordmark at 80% optical size on the bag — small enough that it reads as a quiet detail, not a logo applied.</p>
    <p>The site followed the same logic. Built on a content-first static stack, with a single column of editorial type, generous line-length, and a shop drawer that stays out of the way until you ask for it. We shipped in ten weeks; the team has been editing it themselves ever since.</p>
  </div>
