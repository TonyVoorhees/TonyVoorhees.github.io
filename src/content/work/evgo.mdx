    <h2>EVgo's website was broken.</h2>
    <p>As one of America's largest public EV charging networks, they had a digital presence that didn't match their market position:</p>
    <ls>
      <li><b>Fragmented experience:</b> Marketing and product teams working in silos created inconsistent navigation, duplicate links, and conflicting styles.</li>
      <li><b>Accessibility failures:</b> Missing alt tags, poor color contrast, broken tabbing—basic ADA compliance was lacking.</li>
      <li><b>Blocked marketing velocity:</b> Every form, every landing page required engineering time. Hard-coded components meant weeks of delay for simple updates.</li>
      <li><b>Poor performance:</b> 3+ second load times and a 72% bounce rate.</li>
    </ls>
    <p></p>
    <p>The company needed someone who could bridge the gap between marketing ambitions and product reality.</p>
